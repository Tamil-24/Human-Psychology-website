1. Instant Gratification from Screens

Scrolling Instagram, watching YouTube shorts, or playing games — these actions give your brain fast dopamine.

Unlike reading a book or working on a goal, screens offer instant pleasure with little effort.

This keeps you hooked, and your brain starts preferring the screen over real-life tasks.

2. Children and Early Addiction

Many children cry and scream when phones are taken away — this is not just drama, it’s dopamine addiction.

Cartoons and mobile games are designed to be hyper-stimulating, releasing high amounts of dopamine.

When parents give phones to stop crying, they unintentionally train the child’s brain to seek pleasure from screens only.

3. Rewiring the Brain’s Reward System

Too much screen time makes it hard to enjoy normal activities like studying or going for a walk.

To fix this, we need to reduce fast dopamine hits and reintroduce slow-reward activities like hobbies, exercise, and deep work.

This helps the brain reset and rebuild a healthy balance between effort and reward.
