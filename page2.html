Survival Instincts of Early Humans

Early humans had to hunt, gather, and survive in harsh environments. Their brains evolved to save energy for the next big danger (like hunting or escaping predators). This “energy-saving mode” became part of our brain’s wiring.

When you repeat the same feel-good thing too often (like scrolling, gaming, sugar), your brain gets used to high dopamine.

2. Modern Life vs. Ancient Brain

Today, we don’t need to hunt — food and comfort are everywhere.

But our brain still behaves like we’re in survival mode.

It avoids hard tasks (like studying, exercising) and chooses low-energy, high-reward actions.

3. Dopamine’s Role in Laziness and Procrastination

Easy actions (like watching videos or scrolling) give fast dopamine.

Hard actions (like working or learning) give slow dopamine.

So, the brain picks what's easier — even if it's unproductive.
