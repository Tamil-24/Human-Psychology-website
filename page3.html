1. Why the Body Stores Fat

Fat is not your enemy — it’s your body’s way of storing energy for later.

In early human life, it helped people survive when food was unavailable.

So even today, your body stores extra calories as fat in places like the stomach, thighs, and face.

2. Dopamine’s Role in Food Cravings

Dopamine is released when you eat tasty, high-calorie foods — especially sugar and junk food.

This creates a reward loop: eat → feel good → repeat.

Over time, your brain gets addicted to these easy dopamine hits.

3. The Modern Problem: Eating Without Energy Use

Unlike the past, we now eat more but move less.

The brain still wants the reward (dopamine), but the body doesn’t use up that energy.

Result? Fat builds up, even without overeating too much — it’s a mismatch between reward and reality.
